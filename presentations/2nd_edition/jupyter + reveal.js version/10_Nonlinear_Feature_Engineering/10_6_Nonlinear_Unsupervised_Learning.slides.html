Modeling principles¶

The linear Autoencoder model

\begin{equation} \text{model}\left(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{C}\right) = \mathbf{C}_{\,}^{\,}\mathbf{C}_{\,}^T\mathbf{x}. \end{equation}

is parameterized by the $N\times K$ matrix $\mathbf{C}$ that will act as our recovered basis upon minimization of the cost function

\begin{equation} g\left(\mathbf{C}\right) = \frac{1}{P}\sum_{p = 1}^P \left \Vert \mathbf{C}_{\,}^{\,}\mathbf{C}_{\,}^T\mathbf{x}_p - \mathbf{x}_p \right\Vert_2^2 \end{equation}

We can decompose the linear Autoencoder model into an encoding and decoding modeling sequence:

\begin{equation} \text{encoder model:}\quad\text{model}_{e}\left(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{C}\right) = \mathbf{C}^T \mathbf{x}\\ \text{decoder model:}\quad\text{model}_{d}\left(\mathbf{v},\mathbf{C}\right) = \mathbf{C} \mathbf{v} \end{equation}

In this notation our general linear Autoencoder model is then the composition of these two individual models as

\begin{equation} \text{model}\left(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{C}\right) = \text{model}_{d}\left(\,\text{model}_{e}\left(\mathbf{x},\mathbf{C}\right),\mathbf{C}\right) \end{equation}

To introduce nonlinearity here, we can simply replace the linear encoder/decoder models with nonlinear versions of the generic form

\begin{equation} \text{encoder model:}\quad\text{model}_{e}\left(\mathbf{x},\Theta_e\right) = \mathbf{f}_{e}\left(\mathbf{x}\right)\\ \text{decoder model:}\quad\text{model}_{d}\left(\mathbf{v},\Theta_d\right) = \mathbf{f}_{d}\left(\mathbf{v}\right) \end{equation}

Here, $\mathbf{f}_{e}$ and $\mathbf{f}_{d}$ are (in general) nonlinear vector-valued functions, with $\Theta_e$ and $\Theta_d$ denoting their parameter sets.

With this notation we can write the general nonlinear Autoencoder model simply as

\begin{equation} \text{model}\left(\mathbf{x},\Theta\right) = \text{model}_{d}\left(\text{model}_{e}\left(\mathbf{x},\Theta_e\right),\Theta_d\right) \end{equation}

We can tune the parameters of $\Theta$ (which contains all parameters of $\Theta_e$ and $\Theta_d$) by minimizing the Least Squares cost

\begin{equation} g\left(\Theta\right) = \frac{1}{P}\sum_{p = 1}^P \left \Vert \,\text{model}\left(\mathbf{x},\Theta\right) - \mathbf{x}_p \right\Vert_2^2 \end{equation}

(left panel) A general circle in 2-d (left panel) can be characterized using its center point $\mathbf{w}$ and radius $r$. (right panel) Subtracting off $\mathbf{w}$ from any point $\mathbf{x}_p$ on the circle then centers the data at origin.

Encoder design¶

Once centered, any two-dimensional data point $\mathbf{x}_p-\mathbf{w}$ on the circle can be encoded as the (scalar) angle $\theta_p$ between the line segment connecting it to the origin and the horizontal axis. \begin{equation} \mathbf{f}_e(\mathbf{x}_p)=\theta_p=\text{arctan}\left(\frac{x_ {p,2}-w_2}{x_{p,1}-w_1}\right) \end{equation}

Decoder design¶

To design the decoder, beginning with $\theta_p$, we can reconstruct $\mathbf{x}_p$ as

\begin{equation} \mathbf{f}_d(\theta_p)=\left[\begin{array}{c} r\,\text{cos}(\theta_p)+w_1\\ r\,\text{sin}(\theta_p)+w_2 \end{array}\right] \end{equation}

Using gradient descent we can now minimize the Autoencoder cost, finding the optimal encoder/decoder parameters for this dataset.
